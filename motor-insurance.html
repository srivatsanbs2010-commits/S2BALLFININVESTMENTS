Motor Insurance

Motor insurance is unique because it is often a legal mandate. It generally comprises two parts: Third-Party Liability, which covers damage caused by your vehicle to others, and Own Damage, which covers repairs to your own vehicle due to accidents, theft, or natural disasters. For many, a car is a significant depreciating asset; insurance ensures that a momentary lapse in judgment or an external mishap doesn't result in a total financial loss. Advanced add-ons like "Zero Depreciation" or "Engine Protect" provide granular security, making it a critical component of personal risk management for any vehicle owner.
