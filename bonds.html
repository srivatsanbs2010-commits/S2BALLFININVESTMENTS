Bonds

Bonds are essentially loans made by an investor to a borrower (usually a corporation or the government). In return, the borrower pays a fixed interest (coupon) and returns the principal at maturity. Bonds are generally less volatile than stocks and provide a predictable income stream. They are vital for balancing a portfolio, as they often move inversely to the stock market, providing a "cushion" during economic downturns.
