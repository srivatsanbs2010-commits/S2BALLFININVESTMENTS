Life Insurance

Life insurance is less of an investment and more of a "financial safety net." At its core, it is a contract between an individual and an insurer where the insurer guarantees a sum of money to beneficiaries upon the policyholder's death. Modern life insurance has evolved into various forms: Term Insurance provides pure protection at a low cost, while Whole Life or Endowment Plans combine protection with a savings element.
